TRANSFORMER DATA OUTPUT ANALYZING, POWER OVERFLOW AND BREAKDOWN DETECTING SYSTEM


This is the group research project which i have completed for my third year in Rajarata University Sri Lanka 2016. This group included six members and I was the group leader and could gain lots of team management skills with different group members.

Abstract

Power supply system in Sri Lanka contains several layers and transformers that supply electricity to the domestic users are a major part of it. In order to provide a better service to the users and to extend their service; or in other words to light more homes CEB should keep track record of these transformers and extend more connections. To do that CEB should monitor the transformers status and determine the capacity of the transformer and constantly monitor the problems that arise in transformers as well as in the connections or power cables.
At present there is no specific way to monitor the above conditions remotely or without going to the transformer location or the area where the problem exists. It is not so easy to detect the problem type that has occurred if a breakdown happens in a transformer, the technical team has to search for the problem by the complains of the users in the problematic area. This process is tiring and time consuming. Therefore an IT based system has been developed to detect the state of the transformer and to monitor the problems that arise in the transformers.

Objectives

The main objective of the proposed project is to develop a web based solution that will cater to serve a large customer base around the country using a web portal and to replace the current shopping system. With the implementation of the new system the business is expected to optimize their sales. The overall objective can be achieved by the following sub objectives;

Objectives
  • To implementing a system to gather data from the transformers.
  • To develop a system to identify and handle data losses.
  • To develop a system to identify and analyze breakdowns.
